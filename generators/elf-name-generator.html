About Elf Names

Elven names in fantasy literature reflect the grace, longevity, and deep connection to nature that defines the elven race. Drawing inspiration from Tolkien's Sindarin and Quenya languages, as well as Celtic and Finnish linguistics, elven names typically feature flowing vowel sounds, soft consonants, and melodic patterns that are pleasing to the ear.

In D&D and Pathfinder, different elven subraces may have distinct naming conventions: High Elves often have more formal, celestial-sounding names, Wood Elves favor nature-inspired names, and Dark Elves (Drow) use harsher sounds with more hard consonants.
