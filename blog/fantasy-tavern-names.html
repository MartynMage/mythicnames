200 Fantasy Tavern Names

Memorable inn names your players won't forget

Every adventurer needs a good tavern. It's where quests begin, rumors spread, and parties rest between dungeon crawls. A memorable tavern name sets the tone before players even walk through the door. Here's how to create names that stick—plus 200 ready-to-use examples.

The Classic Tavern Name Formula

Most fantasy tavern names follow a simple pattern:

The [Adjective] [Noun]

This formula works because it's easy to remember and paints a picture. "The Prancing Pony" instantly evokes a cheerful, lively establishment. "The Hanged Man" suggests something darker.

Choose Your Adjective

  • Action: Prancing, Dancing, Sleeping, Wandering, Laughing, Leaping, Stumbling
  • Descriptive: Golden, Silver, Rusty, Broken, Crooked, Lucky, Jolly
  • Mood: Merry, Drunken, Lonely, Weary, Bold, Wicked

Choose Your Noun

  • Animals: Dragon, Pony, Griffin, Raven, Wolf, Stag, Serpent, Fox
  • Objects: Tankard, Sword, Crown, Shield, Anchor, Lantern, Barrel
  • People: Knight, Maiden, King, Beggar, Sailor, Minstrel

Tips for Memorable Taverns

  • Match name to atmosphere — "The Gilded Rose" shouldn't be a dive bar
  • Add a signature feature — Why is it called "The Three-Legged Dog"?
  • Create a memorable bartender — Players remember NPCs
  • Have a house specialty — "The Burning Barrel" serves fire whiskey
  • Include a quirk — The tavern sign is upside down; nobody knows why
