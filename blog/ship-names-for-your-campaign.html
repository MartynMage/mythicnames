150 Ship Names for Your Campaign

From pirate vessels to ghost ships

Whether your party is chartering passage, commandeering a vessel, or fleeing naval pursuit, every ship needs a memorable name. A great ship name tells a story before the sails are even raised.

The Ship Naming Formula

Most memorable ship names follow one of these patterns:

  • The [Adjective] [Noun] — The Swift Arrow, The Golden Maiden
  • [Possessive] [Noun] — Poseidon's Wrath, Lady's Fortune
  • The [Noun] of [Place/Concept] — The Star of Neverwinter, The Pride of the North
  • Single Evocative Word — Tempest, Vengeance, Serenity

Tips for Ship Names in Your Campaign

  • Match name to reputation — A feared pirate ship shouldn't have a gentle name
  • Consider the ship's history — Was it renamed after a mutiny? A famous battle?
  • Let players rename it — If they capture a ship, renaming it creates ownership
  • Add a figurehead — The name often matches the carved figurehead
  • Create rumors — "They say the Widow Maker has never lost a battle..."
