75 Female Warrior Names for Your Fantasy Characters

Discover powerful female warrior names for D&D, fantasy writing, and games. From fierce Viking shieldmaidens to elegant elven warriors.

Creating a female warrior character? The right name can convey strength, honor, and battle-hardened prowess. This guide presents 75 powerful names perfect for fighters, paladins, barbarians, and any woman who lives by the sword.

Norse & Viking Warrior Names

The Norse tradition produced legendary shieldmaidens whose names still echo with power:

Astrid — "Divine strength"
Brunhilde — "Battle armor"
Freya — "Noble woman"
Gudrun — "God's secret lore"
Sigrid — "Victory, beautiful"
Thyra — "Thor's warrior"
Valdis — "Goddess of the slain"
Ylva — "She-wolf"

Celtic Warrior Names

Celtic women warriors were feared across ancient Europe:

Boudicca — "Victory"
Caradwen — "Blessed love"
Maeve — "Intoxicating"
Morrigan — "Phantom queen"
Scathach — "Shadowy"
Brigid — "Exalted one"

Tips for Naming Female Warriors

  • Consider names with strong consonants (K, V, R, TH)
  • Look to historical warrior women for inspiration
  • Match the name to your character's fighting style
  • Think about cultural background and setting
